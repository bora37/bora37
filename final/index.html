Matcha is a type of green tea that originated in Japan and has become increasingly popular around the world in recent years. What sets matcha apart from other types of tea is the way it's made: the leaves are shaded from the sun for several weeks before being harvested, which increases their chlorophyll content and gives them a vibrant green color. The leaves are then ground into a fine powder, which is whisked with hot water to create a frothy, flavorful beverage with a slightly sweet and earthy taste. Matcha is also prized for its health benefits, as it's high in antioxidants and contains a compound called L-theanine that's believed to promote relaxation and reduce stress.
